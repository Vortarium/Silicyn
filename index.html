SILICYN CIPHER - PORT CALCULATION GUIDE

OVERVIEW

The Port Token is a 6-digit number derived from your geographic location. Each user has a unique port based on their rough coordinates. This port is used to validate that messages are being sent/received from the expected location.

CALCULATION METHOD

Step 1: Determine Your Coordinates

Find your approximate latitude and longitude:

- Use GPS on your device
- Search "my coordinates" on a search engine
- Use mapping services like Google Maps

Example coordinates: New York City: 40.7128° N, 74.0060° W London: 51.5074° N, 0.1278° W

Step 2: Convert to Positive Values

If your latitude is South (S), convert to positive: Example: 33.8688° S → 33.8688

If your longitude is West (W), convert to positive: Example: 74.0060° W → 74.0060

Step 3: Calculate Latitude Component (3 digits)

Formula: FLOOR(latitude * 10) MOD 1000

Example for New York (40.7128° N): 40.7128 * 10 = 407.128 FLOOR(407.128) = 407 407 MOD 1000 = 407

Example for London (51.5074° N): 51.5074 * 10 = 515.074 FLOOR(515.074) = 515 515 MOD 1000 = 515

Step 4: Calculate Longitude Component (3 digits)

Formula: FLOOR(longitude * 10) MOD 1000

Example for New York (74.0060° W): 74.0060 * 10 = 740.060 FLOOR(740.060) = 740 740 MOD 1000 = 740

Example for London (0.1278° W): 0.1278 * 10 = 1.278 FLOOR(1.278) = 1 1 MOD 1000 = 001

Step 5: Combine Components

Your 6-digit port = [Latitude Component][Longitude Component]

New York Example: Latitude: 407 Longitude: 740 Port: 407740

London Example: Latitude: 515 Longitude: 001 Port: 515001

Step 6: Verify Format

Ensure your port is exactly 6 digits: - If a component is less than 100, pad with leading zeros - Example: 1 becomes 001, 15 becomes 015

VALIDATION

Your port must: - Be exactly 6 digits - Contain only numbers (0-9) - Be between 000000 and 999999

NOTES

- Users in the same geographic area will have similar ports
- Small movements within a city may not change your port
- The port is used for location-based validation, not tracking
- This is a simplified calculation for demonstration purposes

SECURITY CONSIDERATIONS

- Your port is not a secret - it's derived from public location data
- The port prevents messages from being decoded in unexpected locations
- Friends in different cities will have different ports
- This adds an additional layer of location-based security
